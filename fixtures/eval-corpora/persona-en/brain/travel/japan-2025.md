# Japan trip April 2025

Two weeks in Japan with Ingrid, 5–19 April 2025. Route: Tokyo (4 nights) → Kanazawa
(3 nights) → Takayama (2 nights) → Kyoto (4 nights) → Tokyo (1 night).

Highlights, ranked:

1. Kenroku-en garden in Kanazawa at 7am before the crowds — the single best place I have
   ever been. Went back a second morning.
2. The ryokan in Takayama, Sumiyoshi, an old merchant house on the river. Kaiseki dinner
   with river fish, and the owner taught us the proper onsen etiquette we'd been faking.
3. Tsukiji outer market tamagoyaki.
4. The Shinkansen itself. I am an engineer; I teared up a little.

Purchases: a Sakai Takayuki santoku knife from a shop in Tsukiji (carbon steel, my most
treasured kitchen object now), indigo-dyed tenugui towels, and an absurd number of
stationery items from Itoya.

Hazelnut anxiety level: surprisingly fine. Convenience store labeling is excellent, and I
had the allergy card translated into Japanese before going.

Next time: Kyushu, and I want to see the herring runs in Hokkaido in spring — partly work
curiosity, the SILDRE classifier started as a conversation about that.
