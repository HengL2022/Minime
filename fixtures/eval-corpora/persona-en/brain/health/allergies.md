# Allergies and medical notes

The serious one: **hazelnut allergy**, diagnosed at age 9, anaphylactic. I carry two EpiPens —
one in my backpack, one in the kitchen drawer. Norwegian bakeries put hazelnuts in everything
chocolate, so I've learned to ask "inneholder den hasselnøtter?" before anything else.
Walnuts and almonds are fine; it's specifically hazelnut.

Last reaction: December 2023, a slice of cake at a julebord that turned out to have nocciola
cream. Antihistamines were enough that time, no EpiPen needed, but it was a reminder to stop
trusting buffet labels.

Mild stuff: birch pollen hay fever in April–May (cetirizine handles it), and I'm slightly
lactose-sensitive but ignore it for good cheese.

GP: Dr. Henrik Sølvberg at Bakklandet Legesenter. Blood type A positive. Last full check-up
January 2026 — everything normal, ferritin slightly low, taking iron every other day.
