# Nyckelharpa

I play the nyckelharpa, the Swedish keyed fiddle. Started in 2022 after hearing one at a
folk festival in Røros and becoming instantly obsessed.

My instrument was built by Esbjörn Hogmark, a luthier in Uppsala — I bought it from him in
person in 2023, a 3-row chromatic model in flamed birch. It cost more than my first car and
I regret nothing.

Lessons with Lars Brodin every other Thursday, 18:00, at the kulturskole building. Lars is
Swedish, patient, and insists tone comes from the bow arm, not the keys. Current repertoire
is mostly Uppland polskas: Byss-Calle tunes, plus "Trollrikepolskan" which I've been
butchering for three months.

Goal for 2026: ten tunes by heart, and survive playing one slängpolska at the folk music
pub night at Den Gode Nabo before the year ends. I practice maybe 20 minutes a day, more
on weekends. The strings are Prim, and the thing has 37 sympathetic-and-playing strings
to tune, which is its own meditation practice.
