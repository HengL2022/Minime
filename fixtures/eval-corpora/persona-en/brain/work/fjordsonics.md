# Work at Fjordsonics

I joined Fjordsonics AS in February 2021 as an acoustic systems engineer and was promoted to
senior in 2024. We're a 40-person company in Trondheim building underwater acoustic sensing
systems — hydrophone arrays, sonar signal processing, and marine monitoring platforms for
aquaculture and research.

My manager is Sigrid Halvorsen, who leads the sensing platforms group. She's direct in the
best Norwegian way and protects the team from scope creep. My closest collaborator is
Tomasz Wójcik, our embedded firmware lead.

My current main assignment is Project SILDRE (see separate note), which takes about 80% of my
time. The rest goes to maintaining the calibration rig in the basement lab and mentoring our
two summer interns.

Office is at Pirsenteret on the harbor. I bike there year-round — 15 minutes from Bakklandet,
studded tires from November to April. Free lunch on Tuesdays, and there's a sauna raft
booking the company shares on Fridays.

Salary review happens every March. In 2026 I negotiated a 6% raise after the SILDRE
prototype review went well.
